Pressure increases with depth

\[\Delta P = -\rho g \Delta z = -\gamma \Delta z\]

Pressure measurements

Barometer

\[P_{\text{atm}} = \rho g h\]

Example

If the fluid and blood pressures balance when the bottle is 1.2 m above the arm level, what is the gage pressure of the blood? Density of the fluid is \(1020\,\mathrm{kg/m^3}\).

\[P_{\text{gage}} = P_{\text{abs}} - P_{\text{atm}}\]

\[P_{\text{gage}} = \rho g h = (1020\,\mathrm{kg/m^3})(9.81\,\mathrm{m^2/s})(1.2\,\mathrm{m})\]

\[=12.0\,\mathrm{kPa}\]

Example

\[\rho = \rho_0 \sqrt{1 + \tan^2{\left( \dfrac{\pi}{4} \dfrac{s}{H} \right)}}\] If the surface zone is 0.8 m thick, what is the gage pressure at the bottom of the gradient zone?

Solution

\[P_1 = \rho g h_1 = (1040)(9.81)(0.8) = 8160\,\mathrm{Pa}\] \[dP = \rho g ds\] \[P - P_1 = \int_0^s \rho g ds \Rightarrow P = P_1 + \int_0^s \rho_0 \sqrt{1 + \tan^2{\left( \dfrac{\pi s}{4H} \right)}} g ds\] \[\Rightarrow P = P_1 + \rho_0 g \dfrac{4H}{\pi} \sinh^{-1} {\left( \tan{\dfrac{\pi s}{4H}} \right)}\] \[P_2 = 8.16 + (1040)(9.81)\dfrac{4(4)}{3.14}\sinh^{-1}{\left( \tan{\dfrac{(3.14)(4)}{4(4)}} \right)} = 54000\,\mathrm{Pa}\]

Manometer

\[P_A + \gamma_1 | z_A - z_1 | - \gamma_2 | z_1 - z_2 | = P_2 \approx P_{\text{atm}}\]

Form of Pascal's law

Any two points at the same elevation in a continuous mass of the same static fluid will be at the same pressure

Pressure increases with multiple fluids

Example

If the pressure at B is 87 kPa, what is the pressure at A?

Solution

\(\gamma_{\text{water}}=9790\,\mathrm{N/m^3}\), \(\gamma_{\text{mercury}}=133100\,\mathrm{N/m^3}\), \(\gamma_{\text{oil}}=8720\,\mathrm{N/m^3}\) \[P_A + \gamma_w \Delta z_w - \gamma_m \Delta z_m - \gamma_o \Delta z_o = p_B\] \[\Rightarrow P_A + (9790)(0.05) - (133100)(0.07) - (8720)(0.06)\] \[= 96.35\,\mathrm{kPa}\]

Hydrostatic forces on plane surfaces

Design of engineering systems (e.g., water dams) require the calculation of forces on surfaces from fluids

Submerged plane panel of arbitrary shape

\[P = P_a + \gamma h\]

Total hydrostatic force \[F = \int P dA = \int (P_a + \gamma h) dA = P_a A + \gamma \int h dA\]

\[h = \xi \sin{\theta}\] \[\xi_{\text{CG}} = \dfrac{1}{A} \int \xi dA\]

\[F = P_a A + \gamma \sin{\theta} \int \xi dA = P_a A + \gamma \sin{\theta} \xi_{\text{CG}} A\]

\[\xi_{\text{CG}} \sin{\theta} = h_{\text{CG}}\]

\[\boxed{F = P_a A + \gamma h_{\text{CG}} A = (P_a + \gamma h_{\text{CG}})A = P_{\text{CG}}A}\]

Line of action of pressure force

Sum moments of \(P dA\) about the centroid = moment of resultant \(F\)

\[F y_{\text{CP}} = \int y P dA = \int y (P_a + \gamma \xi \sin{\theta}) dA = \gamma \sin{\theta} \int y \xi dA\]

\[F y_{\text{CP}} = \gamma \sin{\theta} \left( \xi_{\text{CG}} \int y dA - \int y^2 dA \right) = \gamma \sin{\theta} I_{xx}\]

\[\boxed{y_{\text{CP}} = -\gamma \sin{\theta} \dfrac{I_{xx}}{P_{\text{CG}} A}}\]

\[F x_{\text{CP}} = \int x P dA = \int x \left[ P_a + \gamma (\xi_{\text{CG}} - y) \sin{\theta} \right] dA\]

\[ = -\gamma \sin{\theta} \int x y dA = -\gamma \sin{\theta} I_{xy}\]

\[\boxed{x_{\text{CP}} = -\gamma \sin{\theta} \dfrac{I_{xy}}{P_{\text{CG}} A}}\]

Gage pressure formulas

\[\boxed{F = \gamma h_{\text{CG}} A}\] \[\boxed{y_{\text{CP}} = - \dfrac{I_{xx} \sin{\theta}}{h_{\text{CG}} A}}\] \[\boxed{x_{\text{CP}} = - \dfrac{I_{xy} \sin{\theta}}{h_{\text{CG}} A}}\]
