Problem

  • Liquid ammonia at -20Â°C is flowing through a 20-m-long section of a 5-mm-diameter copper tube
  • Flow rate is 0.09 kg/s
  • Density is 665.1 kg/m3, viscosity is 2.361 × 10-4 kg/m ⋅ s and roughness of copper tubing is 1.5 × 10-6 m

What is the pressure drop?

\[V = \dfrac{\dot{m}}{\rho A} = \dfrac{0.09}{(665.1)(\pi)(0.005)^2 / 4} = 6.892\,\mathrm{m/s}\] \[\text{Re} = \dfrac{\rho V D}{\mu} = \dfrac{(665.1)(6.892)(0.005)}{2.361 \times 10^{-4}} \approx 97070\] \(\text{Re}>2300\) Turbulent flow

Moody chart of Colebrook equation \[\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{f}} = -2.0 \log{\left( \dfrac{\epsilon/D}{3.7} + \dfrac{2.51}{\text{Re} \sqrt{f}} \right)}\] \[f=0.0196\]

\[\Delta P = f \dfrac{L}{D} \dfrac{\rho V^2}{2} = (0.0196)\dfrac{20}{0.005}\dfrac{(665.1)(6.892)^2}{2}\] \[\Delta P = 1238407.4\,\mathrm{Pa} \approx 1240\,\mathrm{kPa}\]

What is the head loss?

\[h_l = \dfrac{\Delta P}{\rho g} = f \dfrac{L}{D} \dfrac{V^2}{2 g} = (0.0196)\dfrac{20}{0.005}\dfrac{(6.892)^2}{2(9.81)}\] \[h_l \approx 189\,\mathrm{m}\]

What is the pumping power requirement?

\[\dot{W}_p = Q \Delta P = \dfrac{\dot{m} \Delta P}{\rho} = \dfrac{(0.09)(1238407.4)}{665.1}\] \[\dot{W}_p = 167.58\,\mathrm{W}\]

Pipe networks

Analysis of pipe networks

  • Pipes in series or parallel
  • Conservation of mass throughout network
  • Pressure drop (and head loss) between two junctions must be the same for all paths between the two junctions

Pipes in series

\[Q_1 = Q_2 = Q_3 = \text{const}\] \[V_1 D_1^2 = V_2 D_2^2 = V_3 D_3^2\]

\[h_{A \rightarrow B} = h_1 + h_2 + h_3\]

\[h_{A \rightarrow B} = \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} \left( \dfrac{f_1 L_1}{D_1} + \sum K_1 \right) + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} \left( \dfrac{f_2 L_2}{D_2} + \sum K_2 \right)\] \[ + \dfrac{V_3^2}{2g} \left( \dfrac{f_3 L_3}{D_3} + \sum K_3 \right)\]

Pipes in parallel

\[h_{A \rightarrow B} = h_1 = h_2 = h_3\] \[Q = Q_1 + Q_2 + Q_3\]

Piping systems with pumps and turbines

\[\dfrac{P_1}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} + z_1 + h_p = \dfrac{P_2}{2g} + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} + z_2 + h_t + h_l\]

If only pump to move fluid between reservoirs \[h_p = (z_2 - z_1) + h_l\]

Mechanical power of pump

  • Once we know useful pump head
  • Mechanical power that needs to be delivered to the fluid
  • Electric power consumed by the motor of the pump

\[\dot{W}_p = \dfrac{\rho Q g h_p}{\eta_{\text{pump}}}\] \[\dot{W}_e = \dfrac{\rho Q g h_p}{\eta_{\text{pump-motor}}}\]

System curve

  • System (or demand) curve: plot of required useful pump head to flow rate
  • Characteristic (or supply or performance) curves: \(h_p\) and \(\eta_p\) versus \(Q\)
  • Operating point: system curve and characteristic curve intersect

Problem

  • Water is pumped from reservoir (zA = 5 m) to another reservoir (zB = 13 m) through two 36-m pipes in parallel
  • Commercial steel pipes with diameters of 4 (f1 = 0.022) and 8 cm (f2 = 0.018)
  • Pump-motor is 70% efficient and draws 8 kW
  • Velocity in the 8-cm pipe is 1.5 times velocity in the 4-cm pipe

What is the total flow rate between the reservoirs?

Solution

\[\rho = 1000\,\mathrm{kg/m^3}\] \[\mu = 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{kg/m \cdot s}\] \[\epsilon=0.000045\,\mathrm{m}\] \[\dot{W}_e = \dfrac{\rho Q g h_p}{\eta} \Rightarrow 8000 = \dfrac{(1000)Q(9.81)h_p}{0.7}\]

\[\cancel{\dfrac{P_A}{\rho g}} + \cancelto{0}{\dfrac{V_A^2}{2g}} + z_A + h_p = \cancel{\dfrac{P_B}{\rho g}} + \cancelto{0}{\dfrac{V_B^2}{2g}} + z_B + h_l\] \[h_p = (z_B - z_A) + h_l\] \[h_p = (13 - 5) + h_l = 8 + h_l\]

\[h_l = h_{l,1} = h_{l,2}\] \[h_{l, 1} = f_1 \dfrac{L_1}{D_1} \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} \Rightarrow h_{l,1} = (0.022)\dfrac{36}{0.04} \dfrac{V_1^2}{2(9.81)}\] \[h_p = 8 + (1.009)V_1^2\]

\[Q = Q_1 + Q_2 = V_1 \dfrac{\pi D_1^2}{4} + V_2 \dfrac{\pi D_2^2}{4}\] \[Q = V_1 (0.00126) + (2/3) V_1 (0.00502) = 0.0046V_1\] \[(8000)(0.7) = (9810)(0.0046)V_1[8 + (1.009)V_1^2]\] \[\Rightarrow 124.097 = 8V_1 + 1.009V_1^3\] \[\Rightarrow V_1 = 4.44\,\mathrm{m/s}\] \[Q = (0.0046)(4.44) = 0.02\,\mathrm{m^3/s}\]
