Basic laws of a system

Mass conservation

\[m_{\text{system}} = \text{const}\] \[\dfrac{dm}{dt} = 0\]

Momentum conservation

\[\vec{F} = m \vec{a} = m \dfrac{d \vec{V}}{dt}\]

\[\vec{M} = \dfrac{d \vec{H}}{dt}\] where the angular momentum of the system about its center of mass is \[\vec{H} = \sum \left( \vec{r} \times \vec{V} \right) \delta m\]

Energy conservation

\[\delta Q - \delta W = dE\] \[\dot{Q} - \dot{W} = \dfrac{dE}{dt}\]

Entropy change relationship to heat added \[dS \geq \dfrac{\delta Q}{T}\]

Volume and Mass rate of flow

Amount of fluid through \(dA\) in time \(dt\) \[d V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-_ = \left( \vec{V} \cdot \vec{n} \right) dA dt\]

Volumetric flow rate

\[Q = \int_S \left( \vec{V} \cdot \vec{n} \right) dA = \int_S V_n dA\]

  • \(\vec{n}\) is considered positive outwards
  • If \(Q\) is positive then outflow
  • If \(Q\) is negative then inflow
  • Typical units are \(m^3/s\)

Mass flow rate

If we multiply by density \[ \dot{m} = \int_S \rho \left( \vec{V} \cdot \vec{n} \right) dA = \int_S \rho V_n dA\]

If density and velocity are constant over \(S\) then \[\dot{m} = \rho Q = \rho A V\]

Streamlines

Useful indicators of the instantaneous direction of fluid motion throughout the flow field

Streamtubes

  • Consists of a bundle of streamlines
  • Fluid cannot cross a streamline or streamtube's boundary

Pathlines

The actual path traveled by an individual fluid particle over some time period

Streaklines

The curve formed by all fluid particles that have passed sequentially through a prescribed point in the flow
