What is Surface Tension (BBC)

Surface tension

  • Net attractive force which pulls surface molecule toward interior
  • Liquid tends to minimize its surface area

What is Surface Tension (BBC)

Magnitude of this pulling force is Surface Tension

or coefficient of surface tension \(Y\) (units of \(\mathrm{N/m}\))

\[Y = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} 0.073\,\mathrm{N/m} & \text{air-water} \\ 0.48\,\mathrm{N/m} & \text{air-mercury} \end{array} \right.\]

Surface tension can be changed by impurities!

Surface tension of a curved interface

Pressure is higher on the concave side

\[2 R L = 2 Y L\] \[\Rightarrow \Delta p = \dfrac{Y}{L}\]

\[\pi R^2 \Delta p = 2 \pi R Y\] \[\Rightarrow \Delta p = \dfrac{2 Y}{R}\]

General case \[\boxed{\Delta p = Y (R_1^{-1} + R_2^{-1})}\]

Vapor pressure

Vapor pressure is the pressure at which a liquid boils and is in equilibrium with its own vapor

  • If \(p_l > p_v\) then only evaporation occurs
  • If \(p_l < p_v\) then vapor bubbles begin to appear

Cavitation

  • Reduces performance
  • Generates vibrations
  • Causes damage due to erosion, fatiguing, surface pitting

Cavitation number

\[C_a = \dfrac{p_a - p_v}{0.5 \rho V^2}\]

  • \(p_a\): ambient pressure
  • \(p_v\): vapor pressure
  • \(V\): characteristic flow velocity
  • \(\rho\): fluid density
