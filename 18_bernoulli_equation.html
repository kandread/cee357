Problem 1

If you had a beer keg that was not full but the height of the beer inside was at 80 cm, what is the velocity that beer will flow at the tap?

Problem 2

What is the velocity at the center of the pipe?

Solution

  • Flow is steady and incompressible
  • No energy loss between 1 and 2

\[\dfrac{P_1}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} + z_1 = \dfrac{P_2}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} + z_2\]

\[\dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} = \dfrac{P_2 - P_1}{\rho g}\]

\[\dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} = \dfrac{\rho g (h_1 + h_2 + h_3) - \rho g (h_1 + h_2)}{\rho g} = h_3\]

\[V_1 = \dfrac{2 g h_3} = \sqrt{2(9.81)(0.12)} = 1.53\,\mathrm{m/s}\]

Problem 3

  • In a hydroelectric power plant, water enters the turbine nozzles at 800 kPa absolute pressure with a low velocity
  • The nozzle outlets are exposed to atmospheric pressure of 100 kPa
  • Calculate the maximum velocity water can be accelerated before striking the turbine blades

Solution

Assume density of water at 1000 kg/m3

\[\dfrac{P_1}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} + z_1 = \dfrac{P_2}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} + z_2\]

\[\dfrac{P_1}{\rho g} = \dfrac{P_a}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g}\]

\[V_2 = \sqrt{\dfrac{2(P_1 - P_a)}{\rho}} = \sqrt{\dfrac{(2)(800-100)}{1000}} = 37.4\,\mathrm{m/s}\]

Problem 4

A Pitot-static probe connected to a water manometer is used to measure air velocity. If the vertical distance between the fluid levels is 5.5 cm, calculate the air velocity. Assume the density of air is 1.16 kg/m3

Solution

Assume density of water 1000 kg/m3

\[\dfrac{P_1}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} + z_1 = \dfrac{P_2}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} + z_2\]

\[\dfrac{P_1}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} = \dfrac{P_2}{\rho g}\]

\[\Rightarrow V_1 = \sqrt{\dfrac{2(P_2 - P_1)}{\rho_{\text{air}}}}\]

\[P_2 - P_1 = \rho_{\text{water}} g h\]

\[V_1 = \sqrt{\dfrac{2 \rho_{\text{water}} g h}{\rho_{\text{air}}}} = \sqrt{\dfrac{(2)(1000)(9.81)(0.055)}{1.16}}\]

\[\Rightarrow V_1 = 30.5\,\mathrm{m/s}\]

Problem 5

A piezometer and a Pitot tube are tapped into a 4-cm-diameter horizontal water pipe, and the height of the water columns are measured to be 26 cm in the piezometer and 35 cm in the Pitot tube (both measured from the top surface of the pipe). Determine the velocity at the center of the pipe.

Solution

\[\dfrac{P_1}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} + z_1 = \dfrac{P_2}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} + z_2\]

\[\dfrac{P_1}{\rho g} + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} + \cancel{z_1} = \dfrac{P_2}{\rho g} + \cancelto{0}{\dfrac{V_2^2}{2g}} + \cancel{z_2}\]

\[\dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} = \dfrac{P_2 - P_1}{\rho g}\]

\[\dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} = \dfrac{\rho g (h_{\text{pitot}} + R) - \rho g (h_{\text{piezo}} + R)}{\rho g} = h_{\text{pitot}} - h_{\text{piezo}}\]

\[V_1 = \sqrt{2g(h_{\text{pitot}} - h_{\text{piezo}})} = \sqrt{2(9.81)(0.35 - 0.26)} = 1.33\,\mathrm{m/s}\]
