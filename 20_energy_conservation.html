Energy equation for fixed CV

\[\dot{Q} + \dot{W}_s = \dfrac{d}{dt} \int_{\text{CV}} e \rho d V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex- + \int_{\text{CS}} \left( \dfrac{P}{\rho} + e \right) \rho \left( \vec{V}_r \cdot \vec{n} \right) dA\]

For fixed control volume \[\vec{V}_r \equiv \vec{V}\]

Enthalpy

Remember that \(e = \hat{u} + \dfrac{V^2}{2} + gz\)

Enthalpy \(H\) is the sum of a system's total heat content and has units of \(\mathrm{J}\)

\[H = U + P V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-\]

Specific enthalpy: \(\hat{h} = \hat{u} + \dfrac{P}{\rho}\)

1-D energy flux

\[\int_{\text{CS}} \left( \dfrac{P}{\rho} + e \right) \rho \left( \vec{V}_r \cdot \vec{n} \right) dA\] \[\sum_{\text{out}} \dot{m} \left( \dfrac{P}{\rho} + e \right) - \sum_{\text{in}} \dot{m} \left( \dfrac{P}{\rho} + e \right)\]

\begin{align} \dot{Q} + \dot{W}_s &= \dfrac{d}{dt} \int_{\text{CV}} e \rho d V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex- \nonumber \\ & + \sum_{\text{out}} \dot{m} \left( \hat{h} - \dfrac{V^2}{2} +gz \right) \nonumber \\ & - \sum_{\text{in}} \dot{m} \left( \hat{h} + \dfrac{V^2}{2} +gz \right) \nonumber \end{align}

Problem

Steady flow machine with air (\(R=1716\) and \(c_p=6003\,\mathrm{ft-lbf/slug \cdot ^{\circ}R}\)). Work is provided at 150 hp, find pressure \(P_3\) and heat transfer \(\dot{Q}\).

Section \(A\),ft2 \(Q\),ft3/s \(T\),degF \(P\),lbf/in2 \(z\),ft
1 0.4 100 70 20 1.0
2 1.0 40 100 30 4.0
3 0.25 50 200 ? 1.5

Solution

\[V_1 = \dfrac{Q_1}{A_1} = \dfrac{100\,\mathrm{ft^3/s}}{0.4\,\mathrm{ft^2}}=250\,\mathrm{ft/s}\] \[V_2 = \dfrac{Q_2}{A_2} = \dfrac{40\,\mathrm{ft^3/s}}{1.0\,\mathrm{ft^2}}=40\,\mathrm{ft/s}\] \[V_3 = \dfrac{Q_3}{A_3} = \dfrac{50\,\mathrm{ft^3/s}}{0.25\,\mathrm{ft^2}}=200\,\mathrm{ft/s}\]

\[\rho_1=\dfrac{P_1}{RT_1} = \dfrac{20\,\mathrm{lbf/in^2}}{(1716\,\mathrm{ft-lbf/slug \cdot ^{\circ}R})(70+460\,\mathrm{^{\circ}R})}\] \[=0.00317\,\mathrm{slug/ft^3}\] \[\rho_2=\dfrac{P_2}{RT_2} = \dfrac{30\,\mathrm{lbf/in^2}}{(1716\,\mathrm{ft-lbf/slug \cdot ^{\circ}R})(100+460\,\mathrm{^{\circ}R})}\] \[=0.00450\,\mathrm{slug/ft^3}\]

\[\dot{m}_1 = \dot{m}_2 + \dot{m}_3\] \[\Rightarrow \rho_1 Q_1 = \rho_2 Q_2 + \rho_3 Q_3 \Rightarrow \rho_3 = \dfrac{\rho_1 Q_1 - \rho_2 Q_2}{Q_3}\] \[= \dfrac{(0.00317\,\mathrm{slug/ft^3})(100\,\mathrm{ft^3/s}) - (0.0045\,\mathrm{slug/ft^3})(40\,\mathrm{ft^3/s})}{50\,\mathrm{ft^3/s}} \] \[= 0.00274\,\mathrm{slug/ft^3}\]

\[P_3 = \rho_3 R T_3 = (0.00274\,\mathrm{slug/ft^3})(1716\,\mathrm{ft-lbf/slug \cdot ^{\circ}R})\] \[(200+460\,\mathrm{^{\circ}R})\] \[ = 3100\,\mathrm{lbf/ft^2} = 21.5\,\mathrm{lbf/in^2}\]

From thermodynamics, we remember that \(\hat{h} = c_p T\) \[\dot{Q} + \dot{W}_s = - \dot{m}_1 \left( c_p T_1 + \frac{1}{2} V_1^2 + gz_1 \right)\] \[ + \dot{m}_2 \left( c_p T_2 + \frac{1}{2} V_2^2 + gz_2 \right) + \dot{m}_3 \left( c_p T_3 + \frac{1}{2} V_3^2 + gz_3 \right)\]

\[\dot{m} = \rho Q\] \[\dot{m}_1 = 0.317\,\mathrm{slug/s}\] \[\dot{m}_2 = 0.180\,\mathrm{slug/s}\] \[\dot{m}_3 = 0.137\,\mathrm{slug/ft}\]

\[c_p \left( - \dot{m}_1 T_1 + \dot{m}_2 T_2 + \dot{m}_3 T_3 \right)\] \[= (6003)[(−0.317)(530) + (0.180)(560) + (0.137)(660)]\] \[= 139000\,\mathrm{ft-lbf/s}\]

\[\dfrac{1}{2} \left( -\dot{m}_1 V_1^2 + \dot{m}_2 V_2^2 + \dot{m}_3 V_3^2 \right)\] \[= \dfrac{1}{2} [−0.317(250)^2 + (0.180)(40)^2 + (0.137)(200)^2]\] \[= -7000\,\mathrm{ft-lbf/s}\]

\[g(- \dot{m}_1 z_1 + \dot{m}_2 z_2 + \dot{m}_3 z_3)\] \[= (32.2)[−0.317(1.0) + 0.180(4.0) + 0.137(1.5)]\] \[= 20\,\mathrm{ft-lbf/s}\]

\[\dot{Q} + (150)(550) = 139000 - 7000 + 20\] \[\Rightarrow \dot{Q} = 49520\,\mathrm{ft-lbf/s}\] or \(64\,\mathrm{Btu/s}\)

Energy analysis of steady flows

During steady flow the net rate of energy transfer to a control volume by heat and work transfers is equal to the difference between the rates of outgoing and incoming energy flows by mass flow

\[\boxed{\dot{Q} + \dot{W}_s = \sum_{\text{out}} \dot{m} \left( \hat{h} - \dfrac{V^2}{2} +gz \right) - \sum_{\text{in}} \dot{m} \left( \hat{h} + \dfrac{V^2}{2} +gz \right)}\]
