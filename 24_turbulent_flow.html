Eddies

  • Laminar flow: fluid particles flow in orderly manner along pathlines
  • Turbulent flow: eddies transport mass, momentum, energy to other flow regions
  • Even when average flow is steady, eddy motion causes fluctuations in velocity, temperature and pressure

Fluctuations of velocity

\[u = \overline{u} + u'\]

\[\overline{u'} = \dfrac{1}{T} \int_0^T (u - \overline{u}) dt = \overline{u} - \overline{u} = 0\] \[\overline{u'^2} = \dfrac{1}{T} \int_0^T u'^2 dt \neq 0\] Measure of intensity of turbulence

Shear stress

Similar to laminar flow \(\tau = - \mu \dfrac{d\overline{u}}{dr}\)

Experimental data show that effective shear stress is larger!

\[\tau_{\text{total}} = \tau_{\text{lam}} + \tau_{\text{turb}}\]

Turbulent flow layers

  1. Viscous sublayer: Viscous shear dominates
  2. Outer layer: Turbulent shear dominates
  3. Overlap layer: Both types of shear are important
