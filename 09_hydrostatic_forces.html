Hydrostatic forces on curved surfaces

  • Submerged surfaces are not flat
  • Integration of pressure forces difficult as they change direction along the surface

An easier way

\[F_H = F_x\] \[F_V = F_y \pm W\]

General rules

The horizontal component of force on a curved surface equals the force on the plane area formed by the projection of the curved surface onto a vertical plane normal to the component.

The vertical component of pressure force on a curved surface equals in magnitude and direction the weight of the entire column of fluid above the curved surface.

Resultant force

  • Magnitude of force \(F_R = \sqrt{F_H^2 + F_V^2}\)
  • Angle with the horizontal is \(\tan{\alpha} = \dfrac{F_V}{F_H}\)
  • Line of action can be derived by taking moment about an appropriate point

Curved surface above a liquid

Weight needs to be subtracted from the vertical force component

Example

A parabolic dam is 50-ft wide, with \(x_0=10\,\mathrm{ft}\), \(z_0=24\,\mathrm{ft}\), and water (\(\gamma=62.4\,\mathrm{lbf/ft^3}\)). Compute the horizontal and vertical forces.

Solution

\[h_{C} = 24/2 = 12\,\mathrm{ft}\] \[A = (24)(50) = 1200\,\mathrm{ft^2}\] \[F_H = \gamma h_C A = (62.4)(12)(1200) = 898560\,\mathrm{lbf}\] \[y_P = - \dfrac{I_{xx} \sin{\theta}}{h_C A} = - \dfrac{(1/12)(50)(24)^3\sin{90^{\circ}}}{(12)(1200)} = -4\,\mathrm{ft}\]

\[F_V = \gamma A_p b = (62.4)\left(\dfrac{2}{3}(24)(10)\right)(50) = 499200\,\mathrm{lbf}\] \[x_P = \dfrac{3x_0}{8} = 3.75\,\mathrm{ft}\] \[F = \sqrt{F_H^2 + F_V^2} = \sqrt{898560^2 + 499200^2} \approx 1027916\,\mathrm{lbf}\] \[\alpha = \tan^{-1}{\dfrac{499200}{898560}} = 29^{\circ}\]

Hydrostatic forces in layered fluids

  • Fluid layered with different densities
  • Equations for hydrostatic forces are valid for each layer
  • Compute and sum separate layer forces and moments

\[F = \sum F_i = \sum P_{C,i} A_i\]

Center of pressure for each layer's force \[y_P = - \dfrac{\rho_i g \sin{\theta_i}I_{xx}}{P_{C,i}A_i}\] \[x_P = -\dfrac{\rho_i g \sin{\theta_i}I_{xy}}{P_{C,i}A_i}\]

  • These coordinates are relative to the centroid of each layer
  • Center of pressure of total force \(\leftarrow\) summing moments about some point (e.g., surface)

Example

A tank 20 ft deep and 7 ft wide is layered with 8 ft oil, 6 ft of water and 4 ft of mercury. What is the total hydrostatic force and the center of pressure on the right-hand side of the tank?

Solution

\[P_{C,1} = (55)(4) = 220\,\mathrm{lbf/ft^2}\] \[P_{C,2} = (55)(8) + (62.4)(3) = 627\,\mathrm{lbf/ft^2}\] \[P_{C,3} = (55)(8) + (62.4)(6) + (846)(2) = 2506\,\mathrm{lbf/ft^2}\]

\[F_1 = P_{C,1} A_1 = (220)(8)(7) = 12300\,\mathrm{lbf}\] \[F_2 = P_{C,2} A_2 = (627)(6)(7) = 26300\,\mathrm{lbf}\] \[F_3 = P_{C,3} A_3 = (2506)(4)(7) = 70200\,\mathrm{lbf}\] \[F = \sum F_i = 108800\,\mathrm{lbf}\]

\[\theta=90^{\circ} \Rightarrow \sin{\theta}=1\] \[y_{P,1} = -\dfrac{\rho_1 g I_{xx,1}}{F_1} = - \dfrac{(55)(7)(8)^3/12}{12300}=-1.33\,\mathrm{ft}\] \[y_{P,2} = -\dfrac{\rho_2 g I_{xx,2}}{F_2} = - \dfrac{(62.4)(7)(6)^3/12}{26300}=-0.30\,\mathrm{ft}\] \[y_{P,3} = -\dfrac{\rho_3 g I_{xx,3}}{F_3} = - \dfrac{(846)(7)(4)^3/12}{70200}=-0.45\,\mathrm{ft}\]

Summing the moments about the surface \[\sum F_i z_{P,i} = F z_P\] \[\Rightarrow (12300)(-4-1.33) + (26300)(-11-0.3) + (70200)(-16-0.45)\] \[= (108800)z_P\] \[\Rightarrow z_P = -\dfrac{1518000}{108800} = -13.95\,\mathrm{ft}\]
