Open channel flows

  • Presence of free surface
  • Pressure constant along surface
  • Natural and human-made channels

Flow velocity

  • No-slip condition at side and bottom
  • Maximum velocity below surface
  • Also varies with flow direction
  • Three-dimensional velocity field

Engineering practice \(\rightarrow\) 1-D average velocity

\[Q = V(x) A(x)\]

  • Average velocity or flow depth as representative quantity
  • Steady flow if no change with time at given location

Uniform and Varied flows

  • Depending on how flow depth changes along the channel
  • Uniform if depth remains constant, Varied otherwise

Flow depth in uniform flow is normal depth \(y_n\)

  • Flow remains uniform until slope, cross section or roughness change
  • Presence of obstruction or change in slope and cross section \(\rightarrow\) flow becomes varied
  • Rapidly Varied Flow (RVF)
  • Gradually Varied Flow

Hydraulic radius

\[R_h = \dfrac{A_c}{p}\] \[D_h = \dfrac{4 A_c}{p}\]

Reynolds number

\[\text{Re} = \dfrac{\rho V R_h}{\mu} = \dfrac{V R_h}{\nu}\]

Laminar flow for \(\text{Re} \lesssim 500\)

Froude number

Open channel flow can also be classified as subcritical, critical, and supercritical

Froude number \[\text{Fr} = \dfrac{V}{\sqrt{g L_c}} = \dfrac{V}{\sqrt{g y}}\]

  • \(\text{Fr} < 1\) subcritical flow
  • \(\text{Fr} = 1\) critical flow
  • \(\text{Fr} > 1\) supercritical flow

  • Similarity to Mach number
  • \(\text{Ma} < 1\) subsonic, \(\text{Ma} > 1\) supersonic

\[\text{Ma} = \dfrac{V}{c}\] \[\text{Fr} = \dfrac{V}{c_0}\]

Speed of surface waves

The speed \(c_0\) of a surface disturbance as it travels through a liquid

\[c_0 = \sqrt{g y}\]

Only true for shallow liquid bodies!

Critical depth

\[y_c = \dfrac{Q^2}{g A_c^2}\]

For rectangular channel \[y_c = \left( \dfrac{Q^2}{b^2 g} \right)^{1/3}\]

Hydraulic jump

  • Liquid can accelerate from subcritical to supercritical flow
  • Can also decelerate from supercritical to subcritical flow undergoing a shock
