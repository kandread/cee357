Buoyancy

A body immersed in a fluid experiences a vertical buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid it disposes

A floating body displaces its own weight in the fluid in which it floats

Buoyant force

\[F_{\text{top}} = \rho_f g s A\] \[F_{\text{bottom}} = \rho_f g (s+h) A\]

Difference is net upward force: buoyant force

\[F_B = F_{\text{bottom}} - F_{\text{top}} = \rho_f g (s + h) A - \rho_f g s A = \rho_f g h A\] \[\Rightarrow F_B = \rho_f g V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-\]

The buoyant force is equal to the weight of the liquid displaced by the plate.

Line of action of buoyant force

  • Assumption of uniform specific weight
  • Line of action passes through center of volume of displaced body
  • May of may not be same as center of mass of the body itself

Case of layered fluids

Buoyant force equation can be generalized for layered fluids \[F_{B,\text{LF}} = \sum \rho_i g V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-_i\]

Floating bodies

\[F_B = \gamma V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex- = W\]

\[\Rightarrow \rho_f g V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-_{\text{sub}} = \rho_b g V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-_{\text{total}}\]

\[\dfrac{V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-_{\text{sub}}}{V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-_{\text{total}}} = \dfrac{\rho_b}{\rho_f}\]

Buoyant forces of gases

What is the buoyant force exerted by air on a person?

\[F_B = \rho g V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex- = (1.2\,\mathrm{kg/m^3})(9.81\,\mathrm{m/s^2})(0.1\,\mathrm{m^3}) \approx 1.2\,\mathrm{N}\]

Weight of an 80-kg person is \(80 \times 9.81 = 788\,\mathrm{N}\)

Error in weight of just 0.15%

Buoyancy effects of gases can be important!

Example

Determine the tension in the rope of the crane that is used to lower a rectangular \(0.4 \times 0.4 \times 3\) m concrete block (\(\rho_b=2300\,\mathrm{kg/m^3}\)) into seawater (\(\rho_s=1025\,\mathrm{kg/m^3}\)).

When it is suspended in air

\[F_{T,\text{air}} = W = \rho_b g V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-\] \[ = (2300)(9.81)(0.4)(0.4)(3) = 10830.2\,\mathrm{N}\]

When completely immersed in water

\[F_B = \rho_s g V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-\] \[ = (1025)(9.81)(0.4)(0.4)(3) = 4826.5\,\mathrm{N}\] \[F_{T,\text{water}} = W - F_B = 10830.2 - 4826.5 = 6003.7\,\mathrm{N}\]

Stability

Ball on the floor

Rotational stability of immersed bodies

  • Depends on the relative locations of the center of gravity and the center of buoyancy
  • Center of gravity \(G\)
  • Center of buoyancy \(B\): centroid of the displaced volume
  • Stable: center of gravity (\(G\)) below center of buoyancy (\(B\))
  • Rotational disturbance will produce a restoring moment
  • Unstable: \(G\) directly above \(B\)
  • Neutrally stable: \(G\) and \(B\) coincide

Stability of floating bodies

  • Calculate \(B\) and \(C\)
  • With \(\Delta \theta\) tilt, vertical line from \(B'\) to symmetry line intersects at \(M\)
  • \(M\) is the metacenter
  • If \(M\) is above \(G\) \(\rightarrow\) stable
  • If \(M\) is below \(G\) \(\rightarrow\) unstable

Calculating the metacentric height

\[\overline{MG} = \dfrac{I_O}{V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-_{\text{sub}}} - \overline{GB}\] \(I_O\): area moment of inertia about the tilt axis

Example

Consider a wooden cylinder (SG = 0.6) 1 m in diameter and 0.8 m long. Would this cylinder be stable if placed to float with its axis vertical in oil (SG = 0.8)?

Solution

Force balance \[\gamma_O \pi R^2 h = \gamma_W \pi R^2 L\] \[\Rightarrow h = \dfrac{\gamma_W L}{\gamma_O} = \dfrac{(0.6)(0.8)}{0.85} = 0.565\,\mathrm{m}\] \(B\) at \(h/2\) \[\overline{MG} = \dfrac{I_O}{V\kern-0.8em\raise0.3ex-_{\text{sub}}} - \overline{GB}\] \[= \dfrac{\pi R^4}{4 \pi R^2 (h/2)} - (L/2 - h/2) = \dfrac{R^2}{2h} - \dfrac{L-h}{2}\]

\[= \dfrac{(0.5^2)}{2(0.565)} - \dfrac{0.8 - 0.565}{2} = 0.111 - 0.118 = -0.007\,\mathrm{m}\] Unstable
