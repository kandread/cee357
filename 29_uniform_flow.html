Mass and Energy conservation

\[Q = A_c V = \text{constant}\] Continuity equation \[A_1 V_1 = A_2 V_2\]

Energy equation \[z_1 + y_1 + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} = z_2 + y_2 + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} + h_l\]

\[S_0 = \tan{\alpha} = \dfrac{z_1 - z_2}{x_2 - x_1} = \dfrac{z_1 - z_2}{L}\]

\[y_1 + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} + S_0 L = y_2 + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} + h_l\]

Friction slope

\[S_f = \dfrac{h_l}{L}\]

Energy equation \[y_1 + \dfrac{V_1^2}{2g} = y_2 + \dfrac{V_2^2}{2g} + (S_f - S_0) L\]

Uniform flow

  • Long straight channels with constant geometry
  • Desirable to have uniform flow because of constant wall height

\[\cancel{y_1} + \cancel{\dfrac{V_1^2}{2g}} + S_0 L = \cancel{y_2} + \cancel{\dfrac{V_2^2}{2g}} + h_l \Rightarrow h_l = S_0 L\]

Head loss

\[h_l = f \dfrac{L}{D_h} \dfrac{V^2}{2g}\]

\[S_0 L = f \dfrac{L}{R_h} \dfrac{V_0^2}{8g}\]

\[V_0 = C \sqrt{S_0 R_h}\] Chezy coefficient \(C=\sqrt{8g/f}\)

Manning equation

\[C=\dfrac{\alpha}{n} R_n^{1/6}\] \(n\) is the Manning coefficient

\[\boxed{V_0 = \dfrac{\alpha}{n} R_h^{2/3} S_0^{1/2}}\] \[\boxed{Q = \dfrac{\alpha}{n} A_c R_h^{2/3} S_0^{1/2}}\]

\[\alpha = 1\,\mathrm{m^3/s} = 1.486\,\mathrm{ft^{1/3}/s}\]

Mean values of Manning coefficient

Problem

  • Water is to be transported in an unfinished concrete rectangular channel
  • Bottom width of 4 ft, at a rate of 51 ft3/s
  • Channel bottom drops 2 ft per 1000 ft of length
  • What is the minimum height of the channel under uniform flow conditions?

Solution

\[n=0.014\] \[A_c = by = 4y\] \[p=b+2y = 4 + 2y\] \[R_h = \dfrac{A_c}{p} = \dfrac{4y}{4+2y}\] \[S_0 = \dfrac{2}{1000} = 0.002\]

\[Q = \dfrac{\alpha}{n} A_c R_h^{2/3} S_0^{1/2}\] \[\Rightarrow 51 = \dfrac{1.486}{0.014} 4 y \left( \dfrac{4y}{4+2y} \right)^{2/3} (0.002)^{1/2}\] 

fsolve(@(y) (51 - (1.486/0.014) *
                 4 * y *
                 (4 * y / (4 + 2 * y))^(2/3) *
                 (0.002)^(1/2)), [1.0])

\[y=2.5\,\mathrm{ft}\]

Best hydraulic cross sections

  • Cost of open channel is mostly the initial construction
  • For given length, perimeter should be kept to minimum
  • For given cross-sectional area, smaller perimeter → smaller shear stress
  • For given channel geometry, flow velocity increases with hydraulic radius

The best hydraulic cross section for an open channel is the one with maximum hydraulic radius or equivalently the one with the minimum wetted perimeter for a specified cross-sectional area

Example

Rectangular channel lined with concrete (\(n=0.012\)) and bottom slope of 1Â°

Rectangular channels

\[A_c=yb\] \[p=b+2y\]

\[p=\dfrac{A_c}{y}+2y\]

\[\dfrac{dp}{dy} = -\dfrac{A_c}{y^2} + 2 = -\dfrac{by}{y^2} + 2 = -\dfrac{b}{y} + 2\]

\[\dfrac{dp}{dy} = 0 \Rightarrow y = \dfrac{b}{2}\]

Trapezoidal channels

\[A_c = \left( b + \dfrac{y}{\tan{\theta}} \right)y\] \[p=b + \dfrac{2y}{\sin{\theta}}\]

\[p=\dfrac{A_c}{y} - \dfrac{y}{\tan{\theta}} + \dfrac{2y}{\sin{\theta}}\]

\[\dfrac{dp}{dy} = -\dfrac{A_c}{y^2} - \dfrac{1}{\tan{\theta}} + \dfrac{2}{\sin{\theta}}\] \[= -\dfrac{b+y/\tan{\theta}}{y} - \dfrac{1}{\tan{\theta}} + \dfrac{2}{\sin{\theta}}\]

\[\dfrac{dp}{dy} = 0\] \[y=\dfrac{b \sin{\theta}}{2(1 - \cos{\theta})}\]

\[R_h = \dfrac{A_c}{p} = \dfrac{y(b+y/\tan{\theta})}{b+2y/\sin{\theta}} = \dfrac{y(b\sin{\theta} + y\cos{\theta})}{b\sin{\theta} +2y}\]

\[R_h = \dfrac{y}{2}\]

Problem

  • Water transported at 2 m3/s in uniform flow
  • Channel surfaces are lined with asphalt
  • Bottom slope is 0.001
  • What are the dimensions of the best cross section if the channel is rectangular?

Solution

\[A_c=by=\dfrac{b^2}{2}\] \[p=b+2y=2b\] \[R_h = \dfrac{A_c}{p} = \dfrac{b}{4}\] \[Q = \dfrac{1}{n} A_c R_h^{2/3} S_0^{1/2}\]

\[\Rightarrow b = \left( \dfrac{2nQ (4)^{2/3}}{\sqrt{S_0}} \right)^{3/8} = \left( \dfrac{2(0.016)(2)(4^{2/3})}{\sqrt{0.001}} \right)^{3/8}\] \[\Rightarrow b=1.84\,\mathrm{m}\] \[y=\dfrac{b}{2} = 0.92\,\mathrm{m}\]
